# Korean Apartment Pre-Sale Distressed Buyer Research

Date: 2026-03-26
Subject: 힐스테이트 동인 distressed buyer options
Status: research memo, not legal advice

## Core takeaways

- I did not find a clean "magic exit" that lets the buyers simply unwind because DSR killed the loan.
- The strongest non-bankruptcy pressure points are not "loan failed, so refund me." They are contract-language attack, unfair-terms attack, misrepresentation evidence, and multi-agency pressure against the developer/lender/distributor story.
- The Hillstate Dongin public offering notice is buyer-unfriendly on financing. It repeatedly says loan failure from the buyer's own ineligibility or policy changes is the buyer's problem, and it also says the project entity and contractor are not responsible if loan conditions change. Source: Hillstate Dongin 모집공고 PDF, especially the financing section around lines 1708-1723:
  https://image.ebunyang.co.kr/files/bunyang/pdf/uploadfile_202109035783615.pdf
- That does not end the case, but it means a refund suit needs a theory stronger than "we thought anybody could borrow."

## 1. Creative strategies beyond "wait" or "bankruptcy"

### A. Deposit-refund / unjust-enrichment suit

- A pure `부당이득반환` theory is usually not enough by itself while the contract is still treated as valid.
- It becomes stronger after a rescission/cancellation theory, invalidity theory, or unfair-terms theory.
- The practical structure is usually:
  1. contract cancellation / rescission / invalidation;
  2. then return of paid money as unjust enrichment or restoration.
- Better plaintiff-side hooks to test:
  - `기망/착오`: if there is evidence the sales side affirmatively represented loan availability despite knowing DSR made approval unrealistic;
  - `약관규제법`: if standard terms shifted all financing risk to consumers while insulating the business side;
  - `건축물분양법` or disclosure violations: if any statutory disclosure issue can be tied to a contractual cancellation clause.

### B. "묵시적 구상권 포기 약정" defense

- This is a real litigation argument, but I did not find strong authority saying it is an easy or standard buyer win.
- What I did find is the opposite: a public case summary for `울산지방법원 2016. 8. 19. 선고 2015가단63960` says a buyer-side argument for an implied waiver of recourse was rejected.
- Source:
  https://bigcase.ai/cases/%EC%9A%B8%EC%82%B0%EC%A7%80%EB%B0%A9%EB%B2%95%EC%9B%90/2015%EA%B0%80%EB%8B%A863960
- Working conclusion:
  - treat this as a low-probability defense unless there is unusually strong evidence of explicit course-of-dealing, written waiver language, or repeated conduct clearly showing the guarantor/promoter agreed not to seek recourse.

### C. Challenge the penalty / forfeiture clause

- This is real and worth preserving.
- Key distinction: `손해배상액의 예정` versus `위약벌`.
- Civil Act Article 398:
  - paragraph 2 allows court reduction of excessive liquidated damages;
  - paragraph 4 says a clause calling it "penalty" can still be treated as a liquidated-damages clause depending on substance.
- Official Civil Act:
  https://www.law.go.kr/%EB%B2%95%EB%A0%B9/%EB%AF%BC%EB%B2%95
- En banc Supreme Court `2018다248855, 248862` is the leading modern case.
- Official Supreme Court PDF:
  https://file.scourt.go.kr/dcboard/1678768066640_132746.pdf
- Official Supreme Court video summary page:
  https://eng.scourt.go.kr/supreme/media/MediaActivityViewAction.work?gubun=706&pageIndex=1&pageSize=12&seqnum=146
- Practical read:
  - if the clause is substantively liquidated damages, reduction is live under Article 398(2);
  - if it is a true penalty, automatic reduction by analogy is much harder after `2018다248855`;
  - even then, buyers can still test `민법 제103조`, `제104조`, and `약관규제법 제6조, 제8조, 제9조`.

### D. Unfair terms challenge under the Terms Regulation Act

- This is one of the more serious buyer-side tools because the financing-risk language in the Hillstate Dongin notice looks highly one-sided.
- Key statute:
  - `약관의 규제에 관한 법률` Articles 6, 8, 9
  - official law URL:
    https://www.law.go.kr/%EB%B2%95%EB%A0%B9/%EC%95%BD%EA%B4%80%EC%9D%98%EA%B7%9C%EC%A0%9C%EC%97%90%EA%B4%80%ED%95%9C%EB%B2%95%EB%A5%A0
- Why it matters here:
  - the public notice says the buyer must still pay even if financing fails from policy or personal ineligibility;
  - it says loan arrangement is only a convenience;
  - it says even changes caused by government policy, bank rules, the project entity, or contractor do not create liability against the project side.
- That is not automatically void, but it gives a cleaner FTC / civil challenge theory than a bare "the market changed."

### E. Can Hyundai Construction be put on the hook?

- Hard, but not impossible.
- Default answer:
  - not just because it is the `시공사`;
  - not just because "Hillstate" branding was used;
  - not just because the SPC is thinly capitalized.
- Better routes against Hyundai:
  - direct advertising or 상담 statements attributable to Hyundai or Hyundai-branded agents;
  - express written promises on financing or refund structure;
  - evidence Hyundai controlled the sales messaging, lender coordination, or post-default enforcement strategy;
  - a tort / joint-misrepresentation theory if the branding and sales process made Hyundai more than a passive builder.
- Contra risk:
  - the Hillstate Dongin notice expressly tries to cut off responsibility against both `사업주체` and `시공사` for financing changes;
  - public commentary on Supreme Court `2024다204986` indicates Korean courts are willing to respect limited-liability structures in presale disputes.
- Public summary of `2024다204986`:
  https://lawkorea.net/entry/%EB%8C%80%EB%B2%95%EC%9B%90-2024%EB%8B%A4204986-%ED%8C%90%EA%B2%B0%EB%AC%B8-%EB%8C%80%EB%B2%95-%E2%80%9C%E2%80%98%EC%8B%A0%ED%83%81%EC%9E%AC%EC%82%B0-%ED%95%9C%EB%8F%84%E2%80%99-%ED%8A%B9%EC%95%BD-%EC%9C%A0%ED%9A%A8%C2%B7%C2%B7%C2%B7%EC%8B%A0%ED%83%81%EC%82%AC-%EB%B6%84%EC%96%91%EB%8C%80%EA%B8%88-%EC%A0%84%EC%95%A1-%EB%B0%98%ED%99%98-%EB%B6%88%ED%95%84%EC%9A%94%E2%80%9D

## 2. Recent rulings and case law to use

### A. Supreme Court 2025다215248

- Official Supreme Court summary PDF:
  https://www.scourt.go.kr/sjudge/1766970387966_100627.pdf
- Public court page:
  https://xn--yk3b40q.kr/portal/news/NewsViewAction.work?gubun=4&pageIndex=1&searchOption=&searchWord=&seqnum=10820&type=5
- Issue:
  - whether a contractual cancellation clause tied to a `건축물의 분양에 관한 법률 제9조` correction order should be read literally.
- Use:
  - strong for the proposition that agreed cancellation language should be interpreted by its text, not narrowed by courts adding extra "seriousness" requirements.
- Limitation:
  - this helps only if the buyers can tie their case to actual contract text or statutory trigger events. It does not itself solve a pure DSR-failure fact pattern.

### B. Supreme Court 2018다248855, 248862

- Official Supreme Court PDF:
  https://file.scourt.go.kr/dcboard/1678768066640_132746.pdf
- Core point:
  - not every clause labeled penalty is freely reducible;
  - the real legal classification depends on contractual substance and party intent.
- Use:
  - for attacking a forfeiture / penalty clause by arguing it is really `손해배상액의 예정`, which allows reduction if excessive.
- Limitation:
  - if the court sees it as a true `위약벌`, buyer-side reduction arguments get much harder.

## 3. Government / quasi-government programs

### A. 새출발기금

- Official FAQ:
  https://www.newstartfund.or.kr/Faq/List.do
- Official FAQ lines I verified say support is for people who operated a business between April 2020 and June 2025 and covers business-related loans.
- The same FAQ also says excluded debts include:
  - housing purchase and related household loans;
  - contract / bid / defect / fulfillment guarantee and similar items.
- Practical read:
  - for a personal apartment presale debt or guarantee-recoup debt, eligibility looks poor unless the debt is somehow re-characterized into an eligible business debt.
  - for these facts, I would treat `새출발기금` as probably not available, not as a main strategy.

### B. 신용회복위원회 개인워크아웃

- Official site:
  https://mc.ccrs.or.kr/index.do
- Public search snippet from the official site says individual workout can cover guarantor-backed debt after the guarantor has subrogated:
  - `보증기관의 대위변제 등으로 발생한 보증채무`
  - `신용회복지원 협약가입기관에 대한 채무`
- Practical read:
  - this is much more plausible than 새출발기금 once the debt has actually become recourse debt after subrogation and once the creditor is within the workout framework;
  - it is not a pre-default magic fix, but it matters if the "ignore and wait" strategy later turns into formal collection.

### C. HUG recourse / 구상권

- I did not find 2024-2026 official material proving the broad statement that HUG has "never" directly exercised recourse against individual presale buyers.
- I also did not find a clean 2024-2026 public example of HUG directly suing individual apartment presale buyers in a standard 분양보증 setting.
- What I did find:
  - older public reporting and case commentary emphasize recourse against project-side obligors, guarantors, or those who expressly assumed liability;
  - older press also notes builders can pay banks first and then pursue buyers for recourse.
- Older public article:
  https://www.hankyung.com/article/2010071829141
- Best current fact-check:
  - the categorical word `never` is not verified;
  - the narrower statement "I could not confirm any current public 2024-2026 example of HUG directly suing individual 분양계약자 in a typical apartment presale guarantee case" is supportable;
  - treat the Pressian claim as unconfirmed, not as settled law.

## 5. Pressure tactics that are legally useful

### A. FTC complaint

- Strong candidate.
- Theory:
  - the financing clauses look like standard terms that put all financing-policy and qualification risk on consumers while disclaiming responsibility on the project side.
- Official FTC site:
  https://www.ftc.go.kr
- Best use:
  - challenge the one-sided financing and penalty clauses as unfair terms;
  - attach the 모집공고, contract, notices, and any standardized sales scripts.

### B. FSS complaint

- Useful if there was lender or arranger misconduct.
- Official FSS site:
  https://www.fss.or.kr
- Best targets:
  - bank or intermediary statements about easy approval;
  - failure to disclose realistic DSR limits;
  - evidence that the finance package was sold as essentially guaranteed.
- Weak target:
  - just complaining that DSR rules later blocked approval, without proof of misleading finance guidance.

### C. 국민신문고

- Still useful, mainly as a routing and record-building tool.
- Official site:
  https://www.epeople.go.kr
- Best agencies to cross-file:
  - 공정거래위원회
  - 국토교통부
  - 금융감독원
  - 대구시 / 관할 구청
- Use:
  - create written answers;
  - force agencies to say who has jurisdiction;
  - build a paper trail before media or civil action.

### D. Cross-filing strategy

- This is real and can help.
- Suggested order:
  1. secure evidence bundle;
  2. FTC unfair-terms complaint;
  3. FSS complaint if there is any misleading loan-approval record;
  4. 국민신문고 cross-filing to FTC, MOLIT, local government;
  5. media outreach after the agencies have docket numbers.
- Why:
  - press works better once there is a paper trail;
  - agencies move more slowly when the complaint is only emotional and not documented.

## 6. What I would classify as the most usable strategies

- Preserve all sales representations on financing.
  - Kakao, 문자, 녹취, brochures, model-house handouts, bank intro sheets, screenshots.
- Attack the standard terms.
  - This is the cleanest doctrinal opening if the contract language is heavily one-sided.
- Do not over-rely on implied waiver of recourse.
  - I found buyer-side use of that theory, but also a public case summary showing it was rejected.
- Treat Hyundai liability as leverage, not as the primary cause of action.
  - It becomes stronger only with direct branding-control or misrepresentation evidence.
- Keep workout tools in reserve.
  - `새출발기금` looks weak on these facts.
  - `개인워크아웃` may become relevant if the debt matures into formal guarantor/subrogation recourse.
