# Hillstate Dongin Playbook Stress Test

Date: 2026-03-26
Purpose: pushback memo on the current Hillstate Dongin strategy
Status: legal research memo, not legal advice

## Bottom line

- The current top 3 theories are directionally right, but the plan is too complaint-heavy and not time-sensitive enough on claim-preservation.
- The biggest hole is timing: if a real `기망 취소` theory exists, I would not just "wait." I would preserve it now.
- The second biggest hole is target selection: you are focusing on the SPC/developer, but the public notice names a `분양대행사` and a `관리형 토지신탁` structure. If the actual misrepresentation came from sales staff, the sales agency may be the cleaner defendant than the SPC alone.
- The third hole is insolvency analysis: SPC weakness may help delay, but it usually hurts refund recovery.

## 1. What you may be missing

### A. Immediate preservation of rescission rights

- If the buyers have a colorable `사기/기망` theory, the current "wait" posture is too passive.
- Civil Act `제146조` says the cancellation right expires `3 years from the day the party can ratify` and `10 years from the legal act`.
- Official source:
  https://www.law.go.kr/LSW/lsSideInfoP.do?docCls=jo&joBrNo=00&joNo=0146&lsiSeq=265307&urlMode=lsScJoRltInfoR
- Practical implication:
  - if they discovered the fraud story by late 2025 or early 2026, they may still have time;
  - if a court later says they should have known much earlier, waiting becomes dangerous.
- If the fraud theory is real, I would seriously consider a `내용증명` preserving the position now, not after more months of silence.

### B. Sales-agency liability is underdeveloped

- The public offering notice names:
  - `시행위탁자 유한회사 동인동디엠`
  - `시행수탁자 ㈜하나자산신탁`
  - `시공사 현대엔지니어링(주)`
  - `분양대행사 ㈜삼원알디에이`
- Source:
  https://image.ebunyang.co.kr/files/bunyang/pdf/uploadfile_202109035783615.pdf
  lines around 2618-2623
- If the actual bad statements were made by model-house staff, the sales agency and even identifiable individual counselors may be more realistic tort / fraud defendants than the SPC alone.
- That is a meaningful missing angle.

### C. You should separate "contract attack" from "misrepresentation attack"

- The public notice is full of anti-buyer disclaimers:
  - buyer must verify eligibility personally;
  - 상담 inaccuracies may exist;
  - financing failure due to policy or personal ineligibility remains buyer risk;
  - project side / contractor disclaim financing responsibility.
- Source:
  - consultation disclaimer lines 13-18
  - financing lines 1708-1723
  - promotional-material disclaimer lines 2232-2235
  - trust / responsibility lines 2605-2617
  - all from:
    https://image.ebunyang.co.kr/files/bunyang/pdf/uploadfile_202109035783615.pdf
- That means:
  - `약관규제법` attack is stronger than a vague "they said loans were easy";
  - `기망` gets much stronger only with recordings, Kakao, brochures, counselor identity, loan pre-screen sheets, or a repeated script across many victims.

### D. Consider a proactive debt-nonexistence / scope suit

- If termination has already been declared and the project side is threatening penalty, one alternative to pure passivity is a proactive `채무부존재확인` or a suit framed around invalidity / cancellation / penalty reduction.
- I am not saying file immediately without facts.
- I am saying your playbook currently treats the buyers as purely defensive actors, and that may not be optimal if a key limitation period is running.

### E. 소비자원 timing is more useful than the current plan suggests

- This is a real tactical point, not just PR.
- Under `소비자기본법 제68조의3`, a dispute-mediation application or collective-dispute-mediation application has prescription-interruption effect, but if the procedure ends you usually must sue within 1 month to keep that benefit.
- Official source:
  https://www.law.go.kr/LSW/lsSideInfoP.do?docCls=jo&joBrNo=03&joNo=0068&lsiSeq=277233&urlMode=lsScJoRltInfoR
- So KCA is not just "soft pressure." It can be part of a limitations strategy.

### F. You may be overfocusing on Hyundai "Construction"

- On this project, the public notice identifies the contractor as `현대엔지니어링(주)`, not 현대건설.
- Source:
  https://image.ebunyang.co.kr/files/bunyang/pdf/uploadfile_202109035783615.pdf
  lines 2618-2623
- If the working theory is brand leverage, get the entity right first.

## 2. Developer insolvency: does it help or hurt?

### Short answer

- Mostly hurts if the buyers want money back.
- Sometimes helps if the immediate goal is delay or settlement leverage.

### Why it hurts

- This project is structured as a `관리형 토지신탁`.
- The notice says the trustee is only liable within the trust-property / trust-business scope, while the substantive project obligations belong to `유한회사 동인동디엠`.
- Source:
  https://image.ebunyang.co.kr/files/bunyang/pdf/uploadfile_202109035783615.pdf
  lines 2605-2617
- The same notice says sales revenue may be used for land cost, construction cost, PF repayment, and other project expenses.
- That means if the SPC weakens, refund recovery can get worse because secured project finance and trust-structure priorities may sit ahead of the buyers in practical terms.

### Why it might still help tactically

- A weak SPC may:
  - delay aggressive litigation;
  - accept discounted settlement if it wants to clear tail-risk;
  - become harder to coordinate internally.
- But that is a delay benefit, not a collection benefit.

### Should they check SPC financial health?

- Yes.
- Strong yes.
- Things to monitor:
  - PF refinancing / maturity extension;
  - attachments, auctions, or preservation orders on project assets;
  - tax arrears and litigation;
  - trust termination events;
  - whether claims are being sold or re-papered.
- Public signals already exist that the project financing was being restructured:
  - Asiae reported PF maturity extension and lender replacement involving `동인동디엠` in late 2024.
    https://cm.asiae.co.kr/article/market-condition/2024112707091352445
  - A 2024 KIS structured-finance rating report referenced tranche lending tied to the Dongin project.
    https://m.kisrating.com/fileDown.do?fileName=rs20241125-1.pdf&gubun=2&menuCd=R8

## 3. Timing / statute-of-limitations pushback

### This is the biggest reason I would soften the "just wait" advice

- `기망 취소권`:
  - potentially time-sensitive under Civil Act `제146조`
  - 3 years from the day the party can ratify, 10 years from the act
  - official source:
    https://www.law.go.kr/LSW/lsSideInfoP.do?docCls=jo&joBrNo=00&joNo=0146&lsiSeq=265307&urlMode=lsScJoRltInfoR
- `불법행위 손해배상`:
  - usually 3 years from knowledge of damage and tortfeasor, 10 years from the tort
  - official Civil Act source:
    https://www.law.go.kr/%EB%B2%95%EB%A0%B9/%EB%AF%BC%EB%B2%95
- `채권 일반`:
  - 10-year baseline for ordinary claims
  - official Civil Act source:
    https://www.law.go.kr/%EB%B2%95%EB%A0%B9/%EB%AF%BC%EB%B2%95

### My practical take

- If the evidence is weak, waiting may still be rational.
- If they have actual recordings / messages proving "학생도 주부도 다 된다" or similar systematic loan assurance, I would not sit on that indefinitely.
- I would preserve the fraud-based position now, even if the litigation decision comes later.

## 4. "Do nothing" risk: realistic worst-case

### Best case of doing nothing

- The developer keeps threatening but does not sue soon.
- The buyers remain judgment-free and collection-free.
- Time pressure shifts to the project side.

### Worst realistic case

1. The developer or a later recourse holder files suit for unpaid installment / penalty / damages.
2. Service is attempted.
3. If the family ignores formal court papers, they can lose by default or near-default.
4. If service becomes difficult, later filings can move toward substituted service / public notice service territory depending on the record.
5. A judgment then becomes a collection tool against future wages, bank accounts, deposits, tax refunds, and any later-acquired assets.
6. Once the claim is reduced to judgment, ordinary claim life generally gets much longer and enforcement becomes easier.

### The real operational rule

- "Do nothing" must never mean "ignore court service."
- It only works as a strategy if they are:
  - ignoring bluff/demand letters,
  - but reacting immediately to any official filing, service, attachment, or payment-order process.

## 5. Collective action reality check

- The project has `941` apartment units.
- Source:
  https://image.ebunyang.co.kr/files/bunyang/pdf/uploadfile_202109035783615.pdf
  lines 247-249 and 284
- So `50+` is not impossible.
- But I think your current assumption is optimistic for this specific legal theory.

### Why 50+ may be hard

- KCA collective dispute mediation requires:
  - `50 or more consumers`
  - same or similar type of damage
- Official KCA page:
  https://www.kca.go.kr/odr/bj/br/bjGrpIncidentInfo1.do
- The real bottleneck is not total units.
- It is proving that 50+ people had the same financing-misrepresentation story, same contract problem, and are willing to sign papers.

### My honest estimate

- `50+` is plausible only if the loan-failure problem was widespread and standardized.
- If this is mainly a cluster of individually distressed buyers, the realistic number may be much lower.
- I would not make the whole strategy depend on reaching 50.

### Better framing

- Track `50+` as an upside path.
- But build the main plan around:
  - 5-15 strong fact-pattern matches,
  - shared evidence,
  - one lawyer memo,
  - one coordinated complaint wave,
  - and fast escalation if a lawsuit lands.

## 6. Mom's business angle

### Useful but probably indirect

- Official 새출발기금 FAQ says eligibility includes personal business operators who operated between April 2020 and June 2025, including `휴업` and `폐업`.
- Source:
  https://www.newstartfund.or.kr/Faq/List.do
- That means:
  - if 엄마 has separate business-related debt, the business status may matter for restructuring those debts;
  - stabilizing those debts could improve total household survivability and negotiation leverage.

### But on this exact apartment problem

- I do not think the presale-liability issue itself becomes easy 새출발기금 debt merely because she has a dormant business.
- Unless the obligation is somehow a covered business debt, this looks weak.

### Practical advice

- Do not close the business solely because someone thinks closure is required for 새출발기금.
- The official FAQ already contemplates both `휴업` and `폐업`.
- Instead, map:
  - what debts are in her personal name,
  - what debts are business debts,
  - whether any later recourse claim lands with a financial institution or guarantor that sits inside workout frameworks.

## 7. What I would do differently as a lawyer

1. I would stop calling the strategy simply "wait."
   - I would call it `controlled non-payment with claim preservation`.

2. I would triage evidence first.
   - strongest buyers only;
   - recordings first;
   - counselor identity second;
   - same-script repetition third.

3. If fraud evidence is real, I would preserve rescission now.
   - at minimum with a carefully drafted notice.

4. I would add the sales agency to the target map immediately.
   - not just the SPC, trustee, and contractor.

5. I would downgrade the `50+ victim` assumption.
   - pursue it, but do not rely on it.

6. I would monitor insolvency weekly.
   - PF, lawsuits, trust events, tax trouble, attachments.

7. I would tell the family one absolute rule:
   - never ignore formal court service or preservation orders.

## My honest ranking after stress test

### Strongest

- `약관규제법` attack
