So:
- If it had been 37% of total → 20-30% dump = our thesis works
- Actually 5.7% of total → 3-8% drift = market absorbs it

Oracle's correction was the most important call of the night. He scaled the conviction down from "max" to 0.58-0.60 because of this exact risk. The trade was sized accordingly small. Even still, the downside was less than the absorption-mode framework expected.

**The other piece:**
Even within "5.7% of total supply" unlocks, there's a spectrum:
- Some dump fast (insider sell pressure → cascading liquidations)
- Some dump slow (recipients hold/OTC distribute over days)
- Some don't dump at all (cliffs that get re-locked, aligned holders)

BABY landed in the SLOW DUMP camp. The drift is real (it's down 3% in 11h, a lot more than nothing) but it's the slow drip not the impulse.

**Net lesson:**
I should have weighted "supply distribution speed" higher. Just because 5.7% of total supply unlocked doesn't mean 5.7% of supply hit market in hours. The actual market-impact rate was probably 0.5-1% of supply per day. That's slow-bleed not catalyst-dump.
