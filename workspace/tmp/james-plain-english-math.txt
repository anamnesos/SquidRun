**Your SOL trade today — two ways it could've played:**

**Scale out along the way (the framework):**
- Entered 126 SOL at $89.01
- Sell a quarter at +$9 peak → bank $2
- Sell another quarter at +$27 peak → bank $7
- Hold the last half. Price pulled back, now at +$12 on that half.
- **Total made: $15 locked in, still holding smaller size.** If the rest dumps to your stop, you still keep the $15 cushion. If the rest runs to +$50, you add that on top.

**Grab and hold (just sit on full size):**
- Entered 126 SOL at $89.01, kept full size
- Price went to +$33 peak — didn't sell
- Pulled back to +$12 (where you are now)
- **Total: $12 unrealized, nothing locked in, full size still exposed.** If price dumps to your stop, you lose everything back.

Same trade, same price action. Scale-out banked MORE real money AND carries less risk on what's left. Grab-and-hold left money on the table at the peak AND still has full exposure.

**Why this wins over many trades:**

1. You book cushion every favorable swing. The chop/reversal can't kill you because you've already taken some off.
2. The money you banked can go into the NEXT setup. That's compounding — each win feeds the next stake.
3. Grab-and-hold locks up your whole bankroll on one bet. You can't play the next setup until this one closes.
4. Over 10 trades, compound small wins > one big win. Example: 10 trades of +5% compounds to +63%. One trade of +5% is... 5%.

**The only time grab-and-hold wins:**
When the setup is once-per-week and you can't re-enter. Crypto day-scalping is the opposite — setups come constantly, and locking up capital costs you the next one.

**Bottom line:** Scale-out-and-compound wins long term by a wide margin. Less risk, more captured profit, better use of capital, matches your 24h quota model.
