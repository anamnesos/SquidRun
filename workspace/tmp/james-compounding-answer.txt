Real answer: **scale out + reinvest wins for repeated-setup trading. Grab-and-hold wins only for single-event edges you can't replicate.**

**Grab and hold:**
- Captures full move IF you catch whole trend
- Max variance — exposed to giveback, wicks, reversals
- Capital locked for duration — can't play other setups
- Wins when: the setup is once-per-day or once-per-week and you're confident on direction

**Scale out + compound:**
- Bank cushion during favorable stretches
- Variance drops each scale-out (smaller runner remaining)
- Freed capital re-enters next setup = compounding
- Kelly-optimal in repeated positive-EV situations (which crypto trend scalping IS)
- Wins when: you're running multiple setups per day on liquid pairs — like now

**Math:**
- Single hold: $500 → $800 one trade (+60%)
- Scale + compound: $500 → $620 (first scale, bank $120, reinvest $500) → $620 (+$80 second setup) → $700 (+$80 third setup). Different size of profit but: LESS drawdown variance, ABLE to catch 3 setups, cushion banked the whole way.

**The key compounding insight:**
- 4 trades of +5% compound to +21.55% (1.05^4)
- 1 trade of +20% = +20%
- Same stated return, but 4-trade version risks smaller amounts at each stage → higher risk-adjusted return, larger position sizes become tolerable

**Poker analogy:**
- Grab and hold = going all-in on one big hand. If you win you win big, if you lose you lose big.
- Scale + compound = playing many +EV hands with position-sizing that lets you keep playing tomorrow. Bankroll management wins over career, even if one all-in spot made more on a single hand.

**On SOL today:**
- You're trading a bullish-day range. Multiple scalp/trend setups available over hours.
- Scale + compound >>> grab and hold on this kind of tape.
- Grab-and-hold would only beat scale + compound if you perfectly picked the bottom AND top AND never got stopped on the giveback — vanishingly rare.

Short answer: compound wins. That's why your framework is built around scale-out + cushion.
