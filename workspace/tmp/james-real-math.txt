Let me give the honest math.

**The theoretical case (Kelly criterion):**
- 10 trades, 60% win rate, +5% / -3% (R:R 1.67)
- Per-trade EV = +1.8%
- Compounded over 10: (1.018)^10 = **+19.55%**
- Same outcome as one big +20% trade

Equal expected value. BUT the 10-trade path has ~3x LOWER variance. Lower variance means you can size UP without risking ruin, so actual captured return ends up HIGHER.

**The practical case (your SOL trade today):**

- Entry $89.01, size 126.6

Same trade. SC banked MORE ($15 real vs $12 unrealized) AND has smaller remaining position (63 vs 126) → smaller drawdown risk if the trade reverses now. Better position, better cushion, better flexibility.

**Long-term bias:**
- Every imperfect hold (which is most of them in reality) favors SC
- The only scenario GH wins is if you perfectly time the top close — vanishingly rare
- Compounding (reinvesting banked profits into next setup) adds a second layer of edge GH can't access because capital is locked
- Your 24h quota math favors SC because you need CONTINUOUS earnings, not one home-run

**The answer:**
**Scale-out + compound wins long term by a clear margin.** Not flat, not slightly — meaningfully. Lower variance + higher real captured return + compounding on freed capital + better quota alignment.

The only exception is a genuinely once-per-week edge on a non-repeating setup — grab-and-hold is right when you literally can't re-enter the same edge. Crypto SOL day-scalping is the opposite of that. SC dominates.

Math-guy bottom line: favors us substantially over many trades. Not close.
