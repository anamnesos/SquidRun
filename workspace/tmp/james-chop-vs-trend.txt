Got it. Consolidating your chop-vs-trend philosophy:

**Real moves take hours, not minutes.**
The best long opportunities over a day come across hours of trend, not from chasing every 5-min wiggle. So if daily bias is bullish, the edge is riding WITH the trend across hours — not trying to time each micro-pullback.

**Build cushion during trends.**
When the market is actually going your way (trending), you're compounding P&L. That cushion is what lets you absorb the inevitable reversal section without it killing you. Capitalize hard when the market is cooperating.

**Chop = wait-it-out, NOT micromanage.**
When price is going up and down with no clear direction (the "worst section"), micromanaging every PnL swing = panic, not discipline. Each tick isn't a signal — it's noise. You wait it out until either the trend resumes (cushion grows) or the bias flips (close and step back).

**This fits ride-to-stop.**
Micromanaging = closing on giveback from peak = panic close. Wait-it-out = ride to stop unless thesis invalidates. Same rule seen from a different angle.
