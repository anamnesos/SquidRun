Good instinct to ask. Real tradeoff:

**Same process (what I was scoping):**
- Two windows share ONE Electron app. One crash = both down. Shared CPU/memory.
- 6 agent PTYs (her 3 + ours 3) all in one app = could get heavy on a laptop, especially during both teams' work.
- Easier infra: no single-instance lock to solve, one Telegram poller routes by chat_id, cross-team messaging is free.

**Separate processes (crash-isolated):**
- Two independent SquidRuns. If our trading app crashes, her team keeps working. If her app crashes, our trading keeps going.
- Independent CPU/memory (each handles its own 3 agents only).
- Harder infra: have to solve the single-instance lock, split Telegram routing across processes, build a bridge so our agents can message her agents.

**My honest read:**
Separate process is the version that actually matches what you're describing. Crash isolation is the whole point — if trading crashes, 은별 shouldn't be stuck. If her case work crashes, trading keeps running. Worth the infra cost because she's going to rely on it.

**What I'd do:**
Separate process. Builder scopes the bridge (Telegram split + cross-process messaging). Shortest credible path, not perfect.
