[AGENT MSG - reply via hm-send.js] (ORACLE #28): Research brief on Korean tipster reward framework for counterfeit/tax cases.

Bottom line first:
1. Customs, NTS, KIPO, and police each operate separate reward schemes with separate legal bases.
2. I did NOT find an official source expressly saying one tipster may freely collect from all four agencies for the exact same facts.
3. My read from the sources is that separate claims are possible in principle if the same evidence independently satisfies each agency's own program, but stackability is not explicitly guaranteed in the materials I found.

1) Can one tipster claim from each agency separately?
- KIPO: yes, KIPO's own counterfeit-goods reward system is separate and can apply even when the report is made to police/prosecution/KIPO, so long as the case contributes to KIPO enforcement and is referred to prosecution. Current KIPO page: `지식재산처에 신고 ... 적발금액이 3억 원 이상이고 검찰에 기소의견 송치된 사건` and 5-month application window. Historical KIPO press release from 2007 expressly said reporters could report to `경찰, 검찰 또는 특허청` and KIPO would pay if the case reached prosecution disposition.
- Customs: separate customs reward exists under Customs Act Article 324 / customs reward rules for smuggling and related customs violations. Customs FAQ says the report must lead to investigation and case completion, then reward may be paid.
- NTS: separate tax whistleblower reward exists under the Basic National Tax Act / Enforcement Decree / NTS rule for important tax-evasion materials.
- Police: separate general crime-apprehension reward exists under the Police Duties Execution Act and its Enforcement Decree.
- Inference: these are separate schemes, not one pooled program. But because I found no official cross-agency anti-duplication rule or pro-stacking rule covering these four together, I would not promise cumulative recovery without agency confirmation.

2) Restrictions if the tipster has a personal connection to the case
- KIPO restrictions are explicit and important:
  - no reward for oral-only reports
  - no reward if already under investigation / already handled
  - no reward if the reporter is the trademark owner or exclusive licensee and a compensation settlement has been reached with the target
  - no reward for KIPO/prosecution/police/customs officials reporting in official capacity
  - no reward for public-institution workers reporting facts learned in duty
  - no reward for collusive / bad-faith reports aimed at collecting reward
  - no reward for online counterfeit reports without the counterfeit item or purchase record
  - no reward for anonymous, alias, or third-party-name reporting
- NTS restrictions are also explicit:
  - no reward if the informant used an alias or third-party name
  - no reward for public officials reporting duty-related information
  - no reward for certain materials supplied while the taxpayer is already under audit
- Customs source I found explicitly defines report as one made by a `civilian (excluding public officials)`.
- Police sources I found identify who can qualify (person giving identifying info, evidence, or other investigative cooperation), but I did not find a published police rule in the reviewed sources saying a tipster is barred merely because of a personal connection. So for police I would not state a broad relationship bar without more research.
- Practical read: a mere connection to the case is not automatically fatal in the sources I found; the real red flags are official-duty info, false-name reporting, collusion, or private settlement with the target in KIPO's system.

3) Timeline: when to apply?
- KIPO: after the case is sent to prosecutors with an indictment opinion. Application deadline is within 5 months from the date KIPO forwards the case with prosecution opinion.
- NTS: not before enforcement. Under the current Enforcement Decree, reward eligibility arises only after objection periods are over and the evaded tax has been paid / collected (or hidden assets collected). The 2024/2026 amendment materials also say NTS must notify the informant within 15 days after the eligibility requirements are satisfied and explain the payment procedure / needed documents.
- Customs: also not before enforcement. Customs FAQ says reward is payable when the report leads to investigation and the case investigation is completed, with reward decided by the customs reward committee.
- Police: the sources I found show committee-based payment for contribution to apprehension/evidence, but I did not find a clear standalone deadline in the reviewed materials.

4) Precedent for large rewards in counterfeit-goods cases
- KIPO clearly has precedent for meaningful counterfeit-report rewards, but the public examples I found are mostly aggregate, not single-tipster jackpot stories.
- KIPO press release (2007): total KRW 323 million paid across 107 counterfeit-report cases in one year.
- KIPO press release (2014): rule revision raised the max reward to KRW 4 million at that time.
- Current KIPO page (updated 2026-02-10): per-case scale tops out at KRW 10 million, with a per-person annual cap of KRW 10 million and at most 2 payments per year.
- So for counterfeit-goods cases specifically, yes there is clear precedent for paying rewards, but under the current KIPO scheme the upside is capped and not in the 'very large' range. I did not find an official published Korean case showing one counterfeit-goods informant receiving a huge individual payout above the current KIPO cap.
- Customs is different: customs smuggling rewards can go much higher generally (FAQ says up to KRW 100 million, higher for drugs), but I did not find a public official counterfeit-goods payout example naming the amount.
