Great question. Honest answer: it varies, BUT there are clear patterns.

📊 Three unlock dump archetypes (from the Keyrock 16,000-unlock study + general crypto behavior):

**1. Impulse dump (within 0-6 hours)**
- ~25-30% of unlocks
- Happens when: known sellers (VCs, teams) committed to dump, OR unlock is huge relative to liquid float, OR funding was already extreme positive pre-event
- Magnitude: usually -10% to -25% in one move
- BABY did NOT do this (already past the window)

**2. Slow-bleed (1-7 days)** ← BABY is in this bucket
- ~50% of unlocks
- Happens when: moderate unlock size, recipients distribute via OTC over days, no single whale dump
- Magnitude: -3% to -12% total over the week
- Pattern: drift down with 1-2 acceleration legs in the middle
- **The acceleration leg often hits at T+24h to T+72h** when initial restraint breaks and recipients start adding to sells
- BABY entry was T+0, current is ~T+12h, so the typical "acceleration window" is from now through Saturday

**3. Delayed dump (3-14 days)**
- ~15-20% of unlocks
- Recipients hold initially then sell as token underperforms peers
- Often coincides with news cycle / narrative shift
- Magnitude: -5% to -15% over 1-2 weeks

**4. Non-dump (~5%)**
- Aligned holders re-stake, strong project narrative absorbs
- Token actually goes up (yes, 5% of the time)
