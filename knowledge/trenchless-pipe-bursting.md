# Trenchless Pipe Bursting for Residential Sewer Laterals

Updated: 2026-03-31 (corrected after James field review)

## Scope

This note is for the common residential sewer-lateral replacement pattern on the private side only:

- existing brittle host pipe such as VCP/clay or cast iron
- burst from a house/foundation pit to a property-line cleanout pit
- replace with a continuous HDPE lateral
- reconnect to the existing building drain at the house end and terminate at the private-side property-line cleanout assembly

This is not a ROW / city-main note. If the work crosses the property line, that is a different permit and detail set.

## End-to-End Procedure

1. CCTV the line first and mark the route, depth, bends, fittings, tie-in/wye location, and proposed pits.
2. Utility locate and pull the plumbing permit before digging. On private property in San Jose, the plumbing permit covers the house-to-property-line work. The cleanout being within 5 feet of the property line is a placement rule, not a separate permit trigger. Encroachment / Public Works only comes into play if the work crosses into the ROW.
3. Confirm the host is burstable from the evidence you can actually access: camera footage, sonde/locate path, and what is exposed at the pits. Clay/VCP and cast iron are normal candidates. Hidden repairs, buried encasement, and other subsurface surprises cannot be fully proven in advance.
4. Expose the two pits:
   - house/foundation pit at the building sewer connection or house-side cleanout
   - property-line pit at the cleanout location
5. Shore as needed, dewater if needed, provide a ladder for entry/exit, and create a straight pull path. The entry pit has to give the HDPE enough bend radius and keep the head aligned with the host pipe.
6. Pre-fuse the HDPE string above ground per ASTM F2620. San Jose field practice also requires removing the internal fusion bead with a debeader tool before inspection.
7. Push rods or cable through the host pipe, then connect the bursting head / expander / puller assembly to the new HDPE.
8. Pull the bursting head through in one pass while the new HDPE follows behind. Watch for heave, utility conflict, and abnormal pull load.
9. Inspect the pulled HDPE for gouges or stretch damage before tie-in. If the run was hard or the pipe is warm, allow a short relaxation period and leave enough slack for final make-up.
10. Make the house-side transition:
    - reconnect to ABS/PVC/cast-iron building drain with the correct ARC band / transition coupling for the materials being joined
    - install any required house-side cleanout and backwater valve assembly
11. Build the property-line cleanout:
    - use a combo wye so the riser comes up straight vertical
    - set the cleanout within 5 feet of the property line
    - install the correct Christy box for the surface condition
12. Place 3/4-inch crush rock bedding at each cleanout location before inspection. The inspector checks bedding before approving backfill.
13. Water test and camera inspection:
    - plug the downstream end with an expansion test balloon
    - fill from the low end to the high end
    - hold and gauge-check the system
    - have the sewer camera on site for the inspector to view the pipe
14. Backfill, compact, and restore pavement/flatwork/landscape.
15. Final QA:
    - CCTV
    - required test under code/jurisdiction
    - as-built depth/cleanout/tie-in notes

## Typical Pipe and Fittings

- New lateral pipe: HDPE, usually 4-inch for the burst run on residential private-side work
- Common size:
  - 4-inch nominal IPS HDPE: 4.500-inch OD
  - 6-inch nominal IPS HDPE: 6.625-inch OD
- Common DR / SDR:
  - SDR 17 or SDR 17.5 is the common lateral-bursting lane
  - HammerHead pullers explicitly call out SDR 11 / 17.5 compatibility
- Joining:
  - butt fusion for straight pipe and most PE-to-PE fittings
  - electrofusion where field geometry makes butt fusion impractical
  - shielded transition couplings or qualified mechanical transitions for dissimilar materials
- House-end fittings commonly needed:
  - ARC band / transition coupling to ABS, PVC, or cast iron
  - long-sweep 90, combo fitting, or cleanout tee as dictated by site layout
  - ABS cleanout adapter and threaded ABS plug / cap
  - sleeve/seal if re-entering through foundation wall
  - backwater valve assembly where required by elevation / code
- Property-line fittings commonly needed:
  - 4-inch minimum property-line cleanout riser
  - combo wye, or wye + 45, so the riser comes up straight
  - ABS cleanout adapter and threaded ABS plug / cap
  - Christy F8 in landscape
  - Christy G5 with steel bolt-down lid in driveway / traffic areas
  - 3/4-inch crush rock bedding at the cleanout

## Practical Bill of Materials

Typical counted materials for a standard shallow private-side burst with a house tie-in and one property-line cleanout:

- 1 run of 4-inch HDPE long enough for the full burst plus tie-in allowance
- 1 bursting head / puller assembly sized to the host pipe and replacement pipe
- 1 fused HDPE pull string with enough extra length to trim damaged pull-end material
- 2 transition bands minimum:
  - 1 at the house connection
  - 1 at the property-line cleanout / tie assembly
- 1 property-line combo wye assembly
- 1 vertical cleanout riser
- 1 ABS cleanout adapter
- 1 ABS threaded cleanout plug
- 1 Christy box:
  - F8 if landscape
  - G5 steel bolt-down if driveway / traffic
- 1 bedding placement of 3/4-inch crush rock at the house cleanout area if built there
- 1 bedding placement of 3/4-inch crush rock at the property-line cleanout
- 1 expansion test balloon for the water test

If a backwater valve is required, add:

- 1 4-inch flap valve
- 1 section of 6-inch pipe body for the valve assembly
- 1 6-inch female adapter
- 1 6-inch plug for valve access

If the cleanout hole is deeper than 5 feet and a chimney/deep-cut cleanout is being built, add:

- 1 12-inch tail piece to stabilize the riser in the deep hole

Equipment:

- butt-fusion machine, heater, facer, clamps, stands, and power source
- debeader tool for the internal fusion bead
- bursting rig / power pack
- pull rods or winch cable
- bursting head(s) / expander(s) / puller(s)
- CCTV camera and locator
- excavator / digging tools
- shoring as required
- ladder for pit entry/exit
- dewatering pump if groundwater is present
- compactor
- expansion test balloon and pressure gauge setup

## San Jose-Specific Details Worth Remembering

- City-approved cleanout is 4-inch minimum and should be within 5 feet of the property line.
- The cleanout must sweep toward the city main.
- San Jose typical detail expects the cleanout riser to come up straight and vertical, which is why the combo wye matters.
- On private property, the plumbing permit covers the work. Crossing into the ROW is the encroachment trigger.
- San Jose trench/backfill spec Section 1301 requires:
  - bedding under and around pipe
  - careful compaction around the pipe haunches
  - in improved areas, the top 2.5 feet compacted to at least 95 percent relative compaction
  - surface restoration in kind / per method

## Gotchas

- Wrong ARC band / transition coupling for the material pair.
- Entry pit too short, so the HDPE kinks or drags hard at the launch point.
- Hidden ductile-iron repair, stainless repair band, or spot repair in the host line that stops the burst.
- Failing to expose crossing utilities close to the burst path.
- Forgetting abandoned branches or reconnecting only the main bathroom group and missing a side branch.
- Building the property-line cleanout with the wrong fitting so the riser does not come up straight.
- Not leaving enough pipe to trim back stretched or damaged pull-end material.
- Gouging the HDPE on pavement or bucket edges while feeding it.
- Forgetting to remove the internal fusion bead before inspection.
- Forgetting the water test setup or not having the camera on site when the inspector arrives.
- Calling for inspection before the 3/4-inch crush rock bedding is in place.
- Weak haunch support or sloppy compaction causing later settlement.
- Skipping the post-burst CCTV and assuming the pull was clean.

## Lessons Learned from James's Review

- Trade practice beats generic code prose on this topic.
- Keep the scope tight: foundation to property line on private property, unless the question explicitly asks about ROW or city-side work.
- Do not invent field requirements that experienced installers do not use.
- Give counted parts lists with specific names, not category labels.
- Say the actual material: ABS, 3/4-inch crush rock, Christy F8, Christy G5.
- Inspection timing matters as much as the part itself.
